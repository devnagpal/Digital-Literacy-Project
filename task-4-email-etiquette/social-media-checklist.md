## Do's
1. **Do review your privacy settings regularly:** Ensure you know exactly who can see your posts, photos, and personal details across all platforms.
2. **Do pause and think before you post:** Remember that the internet is permanent; screenshots can outlive deleted posts.
3. **Do use platforms to build a positive digital footprint:** Share your academic projects, volunteer work, and professional interests to build a strong personal brand.
4. **Do verify information before sharing:** Check the credibility of news or sensational claims before retweeting or forwarding to prevent the spread of misinformation.
5. **Do maintain a healthy digital balance:** Set boundaries for your screen time to ensure social media does not interfere with your studies or real-world relationships.

## Don'ts
1. **Don't overshare sensitive personal information:** Never post your home address, daily class schedule, phone number, or photos of your student ID.
2. **Don't engage in cyberbullying or public arguments:** Avoid getting dragged into heated online debates or trolling; maintain a respectful tone even when disagreeing.
3. **Don't complain about your college, professors, or peers:** Venting online can easily make its way back to campus administration or future employers.
4. **Don't accept connection requests from strangers:** Be cautious about who you let into your digital circle, especially on personal platforms like Instagram or Facebook.
5. **Don't post compromising content:** Avoid sharing photos or videos involving illegal activities, excessive partying, or anything you wouldn't want a hiring manager to see.
